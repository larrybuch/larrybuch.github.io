Charting

  • Google Docs is the free, open-source, sometimes more powerful cousin of the Microsoft Office Suite. These docs are fantastic because you can share them, work on them in tandem, access them at any time from anywhere.

  • ChartBuilder is a tool built by the team at Quartz to create simple, quick charts. You can export the charts and open them for cleanup and editing.

  • Raw is a tool for more complex visualizations like bubble charts, alluvial diagrams, and bump charts. These charts can also be exported for later cleanup and editing.

  • SVG crowbar is just a tiny little chrome extension to help you pry charts you create in chartbuilder and raw out into SVGs that you can open in Illustrator or Inkscape for further cleanup and editing.

Mapping

  • Google My Maps is the personal branch of google maps. Great for sharing and creating maps together, or importing google spreadsheets of geographic data to visualize.

  • QGIS is a free, open-source mapping program. It’s frustrating at times, but insanely powerful.

  • Mapstarter is a very simple web app for making very simple static or interactive maps.

  • Social Explorer is an incredible little web app for visualizing census data.
